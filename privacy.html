Local-First Data Storage

CosmoDesk is designed with a local-first approach. All data you enter into the app — including client profiles, appointments, visit notes, service records, message templates, and photos — is stored on your device using a local SQLite database and the device's local filesystem. This data is not uploaded to or stored on CosmoDesk's servers.

The only data that leaves your device is:

  • Crash reports and diagnostic data sent to Firebase Crashlytics (see the "Firebase Crashlytics" section below).
  • Appointment data you explicitly choose to sync with Google Calendar (see the "Google Sign-In and Google Calendar Sync" section below).

Firebase Crashlytics

CosmoDesk uses Firebase Crashlytics (provided by Google) to automatically collect crash reports and diagnostic data when the app encounters an error. This helps us identify and fix bugs. The data collected includes:

  • Crash logs and stack traces
  • Device model and operating system version
  • App version and build number
  • An anonymous session identifier (a randomly generated UUID stored securely on your device; it contains no personal information and cannot be used to identify you)
  • Screen navigation history at the time of a crash

This data is transmitted to Google's servers and is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. No client names, contact details, photos, or appointment information are included in crash reports.

Google Sign-In and Google Calendar Sync

CosmoDesk offers an optional integration with Google Calendar. If you choose to enable this feature, you will be asked to sign in with your Google account via OAuth 2.0. By authorising this integration, CosmoDesk will:

  • Authenticate your identity via Google Sign-In
  • Read and write appointment events to your Google Calendar on your behalf
  • Store your Google OAuth tokens securely on your device to maintain the sync connection

CosmoDesk only accesses the Google Calendar scope necessary to create, update, and delete appointment events. We do not access your Gmail, Google Drive, contacts, or any other Google services. This integration is entirely optional and can be disconnected at any time from the app's Settings screen. Google's handling of your data is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

Camera and Photo Library Access

CosmoDesk allows you to capture and store before/after treatment photos linked to client visits. To use this feature, the app will request permission to access your device's camera and/or photo library. These permissions are optional — the core app functions without them.

Photos you take or import are stored locally on your device's filesystem. They are not uploaded to CosmoDesk's servers.

Biometric Authentication

CosmoDesk offers an optional App Lock feature that can use your device's biometric authentication (Face ID, Touch ID, or fingerprint sensor). When enabled, biometric verification is handled entirely by your device's operating system. CosmoDesk does not have access to, store, or transmit your biometric data. The app receives only a pass/fail result from the OS.

Local Notifications

CosmoDesk can send local push notifications to remind you of upcoming appointments and other scheduled events. These notifications are generated and delivered entirely on your device — no data is sent to external servers to deliver them. The app will request notification permission before enabling this feature.

Information about General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

We may be collecting and using information from you if you are from the European Economic Area (EEA), and in this section of our Privacy Policy we are going to explain exactly how and why is this data collected, and how we maintain this data under protection from being replicated or used in the wrong way.

What is GDPR?

GDPR is an EU-wide privacy and data protection law that regulates how EU residents' data is protected by companies and enhances the control the EU residents have over their personal data.

The GDPR is relevant to any globally operating company and not just the EU-based businesses and EU residents. Our customers' data is important irrespective of where they are located, which is why we have implemented GDPR controls as our baseline standard for all our operations worldwide.

What is personal data?

Any data that relates to an identifiable or identified individual. GDPR covers a broad spectrum of information that could be used on its own, or in combination with other pieces of information, to identify a person. Personal data extends beyond a person's name or email address. Some examples include financial information, political opinions, genetic data, biometric data, IP addresses, physical address, sexual orientation, and ethnicity.

The Data Protection Principles include requirements such as:

  • Personal data collected must be processed in a fair, legal, and transparent way and should only be used in a way that a person would reasonably expect.
  • Personal data should only be collected to fulfil a specific purpose and it should only be used for that purpose. Organizations must specify why they need the personal data when they collect it.
  • Personal data should be held no longer than necessary to fulfil its purpose.
  • People covered by the GDPR have the right to access their own personal data. They can also request a copy of their data, and that their data be updated, deleted, restricted, or moved to another organization.

Why is GDPR important?

GDPR adds some new requirements regarding how companies should protect individuals' personal data that they collect and process. It also raises the stakes for compliance by increasing enforcement and imposing greater fines for breach. Beyond these facts it's simply the right thing to do. At CosmoDesk we strongly believe that your data privacy is very important and we already have solid security and privacy practices in place that go beyond the requirements of this new regulation.
